Barrett Brown, journalist, formerly served as a an unofficial spokesman for hacktivist collective Anonymous, finally walked free from prison on Tuesday morning after serving more than four years behind bars.

The Dallas-born investigative journalist was arrested the in 2012 from his home while he was in middle of online chat after posting tweets and YouTube video threatening revenge against FBI agent.

Brown, 35, initially attracted law enforcement attention in 2011 when he shared hyperlink to IRC (Internet Relay Chat) channel where Anonymous members were distributing stolen information from hack at security think tank Strategic Forecasting or Stratfor.
